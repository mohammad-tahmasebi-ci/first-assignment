Preston has something for everyong

Ribble Steam Railway

The Ribble Steam Railway is a standard gauge preserved railway in Lancashire, connecting the story of the northâ€™s industrial railways, charting their rise and fall, offering the chance for generations to come together, to relax, learn and have some fun

Brockholes Nature Reserve

Lancashire Wildlife Trust nature reserve has the wide variety of wildlife on offer. Walk along the River Ribble, explore the ancient woodland, amble around the lakes, spot a huge array of wildlife all year round

Avenham and Miller Parks

Avenham Park is a Grade II listed public park. With a backcloth of the River Ribble, Avenham Park ranks amongst the finest examples of traditional Victorian parkland. Designed and created by Edward Milner during the 1860s

Level Preston

Level is the largest entertainment complex in the city, with ten pin bowling, laser tag and crazy golf, assault course and private karaoke rooms making it a great destination for a family day out or just a night out with your mates

Antique Centre

One of the biggest Antique and home ware centres in the North West of England, carrying an ever changing eclectic mix of furniture spanning many decades, from the 17th century up to the nostalgic retro era and beyond

Hall More Holiday Park

Situated in a typical farm setting among 10 acres of meadow and woodland at the heart of the Arnside and Silverdale Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty Hall More is renowned for the variety of walks and wildlife

Harris Museum

Known affectionately as The Harris, this attraction is loved for its beautiful spaces, fun experiences and intriguing stories. Stories that shine a light on the people who have settled, lived and worked in Lancashire and Preston, locally and from around the world

Cuerden Valley Park

650 acres of green oasis to discover and relax in, with the River Lostock meandering through this working estate. The Ribble catchment has a hugely diverse range of land uses, including remote upland hill farms,booming urban cities,picture postcard villages

The River Ribble

One of the longest, most diverse rivers in the UK, is a stunning river playing an important role in this amazing area. A mighty 75-mile-long river, starts life as a gentle flow at Gayle Beck rising close to Ribblehead Viaduct, in the Yorkshire Dales National Park

The Flower Bowl

Lancashire's newest and most unusual leisure venue, offering Curling Rink, also bowling, golf simulator, crazy golf, crazy pool, a 3 screen premium cinema, four great places to eat and drink. The place also hosts a range of ticketed events throughout the year

The Guild

The Guild, a welcoming Grade 2 historic pub with heaps of local history and character. Relax in our Beer Garden complete with TV screens, outdoor play area for children. Play pool, darts or join karaoke nights for endless hours of fun

The Lonely People Bar

Located in Preston city centre, with a boutique interior and experimental cocktailsis, is the perfect place for some evening drinks. Enjoy a unique atmosphere for special occasions, sets the scene for catching up with friends or a date night with a touch of class
